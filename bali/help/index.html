This Unicode character app allows you to produce or analyse runs of Balinese text. character apps are especially useful for people who don't know a script well, as characters are displayed in ways that aid identification.

If something is broken or missing raise an issue. For version information see the Github commit list. The pictures of the UI used in this page are taken from a variety of character apps.

About the selection area

Includes characters in the Unicode Balinese block. Musical symbols are not included. Symbols used for Sasak are included in the advanced section.

Controls above the text area

Transliterate. Converts the contents of the text area to a Latin transliteration that provides a one-to-one correspondence between Balinese and Latin symbols.

The transliteration scheme was developed for this character app, and, where possible, uses symbols that represent sounds typically associated with their Balinese equivalents, rather than the sometimes fairly arbitrary selection of diacritics found in other systems. Of course, the complexities of mapping graphemes to sounds means that this is far from a real phonetic transcription, and it doesn't show inherent vowels, but the process is reversible (unlike most transcriptions).

Transliterations produced this way are particularly useful for understanding the composition of text in scripts, where the individual parts are not well separated in the text.

Transcribe. Builds on the transliteration scheme just mentioned by adding symbols for inherent vowels (approximate), by converting sequences representing foreign sounds to a single symbol, by representing multi-part vowels as a single symbol, etc. This transcription may not be reversible. The aim is to produce a Latin-based version of the original text that is a little more readable than the transliteration.
