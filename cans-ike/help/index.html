List of character apps

Updated 08-Jun-2020 • tags apps, pickers, vai

This Unicode character app allows you to produce or analyse runs of Inuktitut text. character apps are especially useful for people who don't know a script well, as characters are displayed in ways that aid identification.

If something is broken or missing raise an issue. For version information see the Github commit list. The pictures of the UI used in this page are taken from a variety of character apps.

About the selection area

Includes characters in the Unicode UCAS block that are used for Eastern Canadian Inuktitut.

There are two ways of accessing the characters: (a) through a 2-click selector based on shapes, and (b) from a table. To make the 2-click selector visible, click on S in the vertical grey panel to the left.

A couple of infrequently used characters are squeezed into gaps in the matrix.

Controls above the text area

Transliterate. Converts the contents of the text area to a Latin transliteration that provides a one-to-one correspondence between Inuktitut and Latin symbols.

The transliteration scheme was developed for this character app, and, where possible, uses symbols that represent sounds typically associated with their Inuktitut equivalents. Of course, the complexities of mapping graphemes to sounds means that this is far from a real phonetic transcription but the process is reversible (unlike most transcriptions).

Transliterations produced this way are particularly useful for understanding the composition of text in scripts.

Make vocab. This helps speed up the creation of entries for the vocabulary app. You can create a vocab item with up to 3 parts, delimited by |, in the following order: [1] native text, [2] meaning, [3] notes (optional). Much of the time, only the first 2 items are needed.

The text will be replaced with the following:

native_text|meaning|IPA_transcription(|notes)

ᐅᒥᐊᖅ|boat

will produce:

ᐅᒥᐊᖅ|boat|umiaq

The app will automatically generate a Latin transcription in the notes location if the Latin differs from the IPA.

ᐅᒥᐊᖓ|his boat

will produce:

ᐅᒥᐊᖓ|his boat|umiaŋa|umianga

Where needed, the app will add a transcription to existing notes.

ᓇᑯᕐᒦᒃ|thank you|(South Qikiqtaaluk)

will produce:

ᓇᑯᕐᒦᒃ|thank you|nakuʁmiːk|nakurmiik (South Qikiqtaaluk)

The app will also, simultaneously, produce markup for examples in the secondary text area (see below).

If you have more than one line of text in the text area, you must highlight the row you want to convert.

Transcribe to IPA. Converts Inuktitut text to a rough IPA transcription. You may occasionally need to tweak the result. For the ᓯᓱᓴ series this produces s, rather than h.

Transcribe to Latin. Converts Inuktitut text to the standard Latin transcription. You may occasionally need to tweak the result. Like the IPA transcription, for the ᓯᓱᓴ series this produces s, rather than h.
