List of character apps

Updated 14-Nov-2020

This Unicode character app allows you to produce or analyse runs of Ethiopic text. character apps are especially useful for people who don't know a script well, as characters are displayed in ways that aid identification.

If something is broken or missing raise an issue. For version information see the Github commit list. The pictures of the UI used in this page are taken from a variety of character apps

Inputting characters

The app includes all the characters in the Unicode Ethiopic, Ethiopic Supplement, Ethiopic Extended and Ethiopic Extended-A blocks. The extended repertoire is initially hidden, but can be revealed using a selection in the input aids, to the left side..

To analyse text, copy & paste it into the large text area and select the desired control(s).

To produce Ethiopic text in the text area, click on an orange character, and then on one of the grey characters displayed.

Click on EXT to toggle on or off the extension characters. The basic repertoire covers characters needed for Amharic, Tigrinya, Tigre, and Blin. The extension characters are for writing the sounds of other languages, such as Me'en, Sebatbeit, Gamo-Gofa-Dawro, Basketo, Gumuz, etc..

Transliterate. Converts the contents of the text area to a Latin transliteration that provides a one-to-one correspondence between Ethiopic and Latin symbols.

The transliteration scheme was developed for this character app, and, where possible, uses symbols that represent sounds typically associated with their Ethiopic equivalents, rather than the sometimes fairly arbitrary selection of diacritics found in other systems. Of course, the complexities of mapping graphemes to sounds means that this is far from a real phonetic transcription, and it doesn't show vowels for unvowelled text, but the process is reversible (unlike most transcriptions).

Transliterations produced this way are particularly useful for understanding the composition of text in scripts, where the individual parts are not well separated in the text.

Vocab to markup. Converts a vocab entry to markup that can be used for an example. Must be applied to one line at a time.

[S] Show hints. When this is on, transliterations are shown for the syllabic characters. This helps people less familiary with the Ethiopic script to find characters.
