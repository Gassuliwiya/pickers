This Unicode character app allows you to produce or analyse runs of Syriac text. character apps are especially useful for people who don't know a script well, as characters are displayed in ways that aid identification.

If something is broken or missing raise an issue. For version information see the Github commit list. The pictures of the UI used in this page are taken from a variety of character apps.

About the app

Includes all the characters in the Unicode Syriac block, and several from the Arabic and Combining Diacritical Marks blocks.

Controls above the text area

Transliterate. Converts the contents of the text area to a Latin transliteration that provides a one-to-one correspondence between Syriac and Latin symbols.

The transliteration scheme was developed for this character app, and, where possible, uses symbols that represent sounds typically associated with their Syriac equivalents, rather than the sometimes fairly arbitrary selection of diacritics found in other systems. Of course, the complexities of mapping graphemes to sounds means that this is far from a real phonetic transcription, and it doesn't show short vowels in unvowelled text, but the process is reversible (unlike most transcriptions).

Transliterations produced this way are particularly useful for understanding the composition of text in scripts, where the individual parts are not well separated in the text.
