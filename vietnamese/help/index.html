This Unicode character picker allows you to produce or analyse runs of Vietnamese text. Character pickers are especially useful for people who don't know a script well, as characters are displayed in ways that aid identification.

If something is broken or missing raise an issue. For version information see the Github commit list.

About the chart

Includes characters needed for Vietnamese from the Unicode Latin blocks.

Controls above the text area

North/South Việt to IPA. These two features allow you to convert from Vietnamese text to IPA transcriptions. One produces a rough phonetic transcription for the text assuming that the speaker is from North Vietnam. The other does the same thing, but for southern speakers. The output is only intended to approximately reflect actual pronunciation!

Input aids

Type characters. Displays a panel of lowercase Latin characters you are likely to need for typing Vietnamese, and sets up the Latin type-in mode. (This can be switched on/off by hitting the ` key.)

As you type, suggestions for input will appear at the bottom of the text area. To insert one of those items into the text area at the cursor position, type the appropriate number key, or click directly on the item you want to insert. The character you typed will be replaced by the item you selected. (If you already see the character you want, just continue typing.)

Keys that produce such suggestions are highlighted in orange in a panel that appears below the text area. You can also click on the items in that panel in order to insert them.

In general, alternative characters are associated with a key on the keyboard that resembles them when displayed – eg. ɣ will usually be brought up by clicking on the 'y' key (rather than, say 'g').
